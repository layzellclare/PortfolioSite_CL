Overview

Facts

Modern weather forecast apps often display huge amounts of data and much of the time this is extraneous to the needs of the user. Therefore, it is not uncommon for users to switch from a general weather app for every day use to a more detailed one for water sports.

Water Sports weather apps generally contain overly technical data which is poorly displayed, making them difficult to read for novices and on small devices.

Problem Statement

Weather forecasting apps and websites are in abundance. Most are used for general weather forecasting, but for those in need of more detailed information, there are a plethora of water sport specific sites at hand. That said, most are specific to 1 sport and often require membership in order to access the most useful information.

Solution

Design an app that combines the simplicity and ease of use employed in general weather apps with some of the detail seen in more technical water sports apps.

Water sports enthusiasts are creatures of habit, that said, they love nothing more than a new adventure.

User Flows

In response to the user testing methods employed, the analysis of findings and the resulting user personas, 3 user flows were employed for wireframing and further testing.

Joe might have a day off at the weekend and wants to know the forecast...Check the weather forecast at a chosen location in the future

Fern is planning a holiday and wants to keep an eye on the weather at the destination...Search for a location, save to favourites and check if successful

Cam is keen to find a new route to sail this weekend...Search for a new location via filter (Sport/Distance/Body of water)

Style Guide

Considering the fact I want this app to appeal to a wide age range, to varied proficiency levels, to be used easily on a mobile phone and to be easily distinguishable from competitor apps, I decided on the following:

  • Design simple iconography
  • Limit the amount of data on any one screen
  • Incorporate a more natural looking colour palette

Design Iterations

With every iteration, the design become more polished and user friendly. This process can of course continue, but feedback suggests all key issues at this stage have been responded to and overcome.

Removal of icon explanatory text from the profile set up pages and hourly/daily weather forecast screens, replaced by hover indicators on a mobile and button.

Information requests (profile set up) placed on one scrollable page were replaced by a series of screens. The resulting screens are far easier to read and giver the user the opportunity to skip the request's if desired.

Removing the background map on many of the set up pages helped the contrast and overall visual clarity on these screens, as did giving input fields and buttons more padding.

Key Takeaway

From the offset, I wanted this app to be relevant to the widest possible spectrum of users, which in all truth may deem it generic to the more accomplished in their chosen water sport. However, I believe the app is successful in providing general weather as well as water sports weather data and ultimately removes the need to use more than one app for both purposes.

What Next?

Given time, I would like to concentrate my efforts on adding an alerts/notifications feature. This area was mentioned by interviewees during the early stages of the project, but unfortunately never became a reality. I would also relish the opportunity to incorporate 3d features, which I believe would elevate the app enormously. This could be achieved by animating the main weather icon/s and/or the water sport icons.
